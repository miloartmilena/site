According to the Christian calendar, exactly 1965 years after the year “zero”, I came into this world. I can't say for sure if this is my first time here. Sometimes everything seems very familiar to me and sometimes very strange.

I was born an ugly duckling on an “Animal Farm” that has since been violently broken up. As soon as I got to know all the letters, I read everything I could get my hands on and, above all, I devoured the fairy tales.

The tale of the ugly duckling was my favorite fairy tale, and I cried every time I read it. I still love fairy tales. I don't have to cry anymore, but I know why I cried so much back then.

LADA

The ancient Slavic goddess of spring and love.


In 2017 I moved my studio from Switzerland to Serbia and became a freelance artist. In my host country I was confronted with a lot of litter on the streets. Everything is so clean and tidy in Switzerland. But I know that appearances are deceptive. The waste may not be dumped directly on the roadside, but as a "high standard of living society" we are incredible consumers and polluters.But I didn't want to make criticism-art and decided to make art out of the plastic bottles that were lying around everywhere.

The idea of sculpting from plastic waste was born.

am I good?


Progress cannot be stopped, and people do not like to forgo. Maybe forgo isn't the answer either. So far, we have copied so much from nature in technology. And the most ingenious invention of nature is the cycle. Everything goes back into the cycle. Here, too, we should copy nature and create our own cycle for our overproduction of all kinds of things. So I try to make my contribution to this cycle artistically. I give a discarded plastic bottle a new spiritual and material value and thus a worthy place in this world again.

Hermit, Lada and Harlequin


In May 2022 we exhibited with the artist group "inside-outside" in Winterthur in Hall 710. Our theme was “caminos”, the paths. For this title I decided to examine the "inner ways of a person" and exhibited the three sculptures. Hermit for transience, Harlequin for liberation and Lada for the transformation that follows. When the human seriously seeks, he encounters the impermanence of everything. This moment makes us realize that there is a big difference between knowing and experiencing impermanence. When we have grown to this realization, our liberation begins. The harlequin takes it with humor, does a headstand and empties his pockets. In this way he symbolically frees himself from heavy thoughts and any ballast.

“dance with me”


In 2021 I applied for an exhibition in the USA. The exhibition was organized by a non-profit organization called ” Embracing Our Differences”, based in Sarasota, Florida, which uses the power of arts and education to expand consciousness and open the heart to celebrate the diversity of the human family.” Quote from their website. The topic was also “embracing our differences”. I was not accepted, but one of my favorite sculptures "dance with me" was created. I was inspired by folk dances where everyone dances together in a circle. I imagine our ancient ancestors dancing like this to charge themselves with courage and energy. So, my little sculpture represents such a dance. Each dancer has its advantages and disadvantages. Like the angel in the middle. He's missing a leg, which is probably a major handicap for dancing. But he is supported by the others. But he has wings, and his fellow dancers also benefit from this lightness and encouragement.

We should dance together, not fight.
